Overview

The people from across the globe visits India due to numerous reasons: the culture, the food, the weather. The rich culture and heritage impelled people to consider India at the top of tourist priority list. The other factor that propelled an Individual, outside the country, to visit India is medical tourism.

With the availability of state-of-art technologies and global healthcare costs are sky-rocketing, the world can take a leaf or two from the Indian healthcare sector, which provides high quality healthcare at NABH (Institutional member of ISQUA) facilities at highly competitive price as compare to other similar healthcare facility around the world. In this research, a brief overview about the Indian Healthcare Industry is discussed, its scope in the world market, opportunities, and present standpoint of Indian Medical Tourism across the globe.

Why Medical Tourism in India?

The advent of digitization and culture of consumerism have given rise to a propagation shift from obtaining healthcare inside their homeland to other countries. People are ready to travel in search of better quality and affordable health options. Being one of the competes and standing in the line of developing countries, India offers the best in terms of infrastructure, technology, specialist doctors and nurses. It is also the house for largest pools of doctors and paramedics in South Asia and catering the unique blend and varieties of medical services such as Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Siddha, and other widely accepted alternative treatment procedures in much geography. In recent times, India has become the traveler's choice for medical tourism. Read more

Attractions in Finding Healthcare in India

Medical treatment in India include reduced costs, the availability of latest medical technologies, and a growing compliance on international quality standards, doctors trained in western countries including the United States and the United Kingdom, as well as English-speaking personnel, due to which foreigners are less likely to face language barrier in India.

Key aspects that have a major bearing on the ‘tourist friendliness’ of a country includes aviation infrastructure, amenities at the airport, proper transport infrastructure, affordable accommodation, good food, adequate cleanliness, hygiene, safe drinking water and overall safety concern.

Quality & Assurance

India has 39 JCI & 403 NABH accredited hospitals. However, for a patient traveling to India, it is important to find the optimal doctor-hospital combination. After the patient has been treated, the patient has the option of either recuperating in the hospital or at a paid accommodation nearby. Many hospitals also give the option of continuing the treatment through telemedicine.

Apart from that, numerous private hospitals and medical centers in the similar space follow critical hygiene principles and practices, ensuring the overall safety of the patients as well as visitors inside the hospital.

Statistics

Russia Beyond notes that of the estimated 300,000 Russian medical tourists who travel worldwide every year for healthcare, India receives over 70,000 of them.

Aviation infrastructure is critical since it is a major mode of entry for inbound medical tourist. India has good connectivity at most of the airports in metro cities however, ease of service at airport is a concern, Ease of services at the airport is important for making sure that medical tourists have a hassle-free entry as well as departure.
